This website has been created to educate people on the growing declines of animals species on Earth and to highlight how humans are threatening species survival.

Who are the WWF?

The World Wildlife Fund is the world’s leading independent conservation organisation. Their mission is to create a world where people and wildlife can thrive together. WWF have six goals to achieve their mission of living in harmony with wildlife. These are: Living sustainably, reducing carbon emissions, trading sustainable seafood and timber, maintaining river ecosystems, sustaining forests and oceans and restoring wildlife.

What do they do?

WWF work with communities, businesses and governments to promote and encourage people to live a sustainable lifestyle and use of resources. They also help communities protect wildlife and prevent human-wildlife conflict.

Where do they work?

WWF work on every continent to halt and reverse dramatic declines in wildlife numbers and the unsustainable use of our natural resources. You’ll find their workers in every environment across the world working with communities in the Amazon, Antarctic, Arctic, Masai-Mara, Serengeti, the Himalayas and across the oceans, seas and rivers.

What can you do to help?

To get involved with WWF you can adopt one of their monitored animals, donate money, campaign and fundraise or take part in their educational activities in schools to inspire the next generation.
